Welcome to Cheyenne, "The Magic City of the Plains"

Cheyenne, the capital and most populous city of Wyoming, is a vibrant and dynamic community located in the southeastern part of the state. Known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and economic significance, Cheyenne offers a unique blend of Western charm and modern amenities. Below is a detailed overview of Cheyenne's key aspects:

Cheyenne, the capital and most populous city of Wyoming, is located in the southeastern part of the state. It is the easternmost major city in Wyoming, positioned near the borders of Colorado and Nebraska. As of the 2020 census, it has a population of 65,132 residents (U.S. Census Bureau, 2020). The city was founded in 1867 and incorporated on August 8 of that year. Its establishment was closely tied to the westward expansion of the Union Pacific Railroad, which reached Cheyenne in November 1867 (City of Cheyenne, 2022). The city's rapid growth during this period earned it the nickname "Magic City of the Plains" (Wyoming State Archives, 2021).

Geographically, Cheyenne is situated in Laramie County and serves as the county seat. Its location at the crossroads of Interstate 25 (north-south) and Interstate 80 (east-west) makes it a key transportation hub in the region (Wyoming Department of Transportation, 2023). The city lies on the western edge of the Great Plains, just east of the Rocky Mountains, providing a unique blend of prairie and mountainous landscapes. This strategic positioning has played a significant role in the city's development as a center for commerce, transportation, and military presence (Wyoming Geographic Alliance, 2022).

As the state capital and largest city in Wyoming, Cheyenne is classified as an urban area. It boasts a diverse economy with key sectors including government services, transportation, and manufacturing (City of Cheyenne Economic Development, 2023). The presence of F.E. Warren Air Force Base, one of the oldest military installations in the U.S., along with various state government agencies, contributes significantly to its urban character and economic activity (F.E. Warren AFB, 2023). Additionally, Cheyenne hosts the Cheyenne Frontier Days, one of the largest rodeos in the world, attracting thousands of visitors and boosting the local economy (Cheyenne Frontier Days, 2023).

In terms of income, data from the 2015-2019 American Community Survey indicates that Cheyenne's residents have a median household income of $64,598 and a per capita income of $35,637 (U.S. Census Bureau, 2019). Comparatively, the median household income for the entire state of Wyoming during the same period was approximately $62,268, with a per capita income of around $32,000. This suggests that Cheyenne's income levels are slightly higher than the state average, reflecting its role as an economic center in Wyoming and the stability provided by government and military employment (American Community Survey, 2019).

Did You Know?

Cheyenne is the capital and most populous city of Wyoming, founded in 1867.

It was nicknamed the "Magic City of the Plains" due to its rapid growth after the arrival of the Union Pacific Railroad.

Cheyenne Frontier Days, one of the world's largest outdoor rodeos, has been held annually since 1897.

F.E. Warren Air Force Base, located in Cheyenne, is one of the oldest military installations in the U.S.

The Wyoming State Capitol, a National Historic Landmark, was built in 1890 and features a gold-leaf dome.
